Financial Exclusion Prediction with Explainable AI

This application demonstrates how machine learning models can predict financial exclusion based on demographic and behavioral features. It also provides explainability through LIME (Local Interpretable Model-agnostic Explanations) to help understand why predictions are made.

Understanding Financial Exclusion

Financial exclusion refers to a situation where individuals lack access to appropriate financial products and services. This app implements models to predict whether someone is likely to be financially excluded based on:

  • Demographic features: age, gender, education level, residence type, etc.
  • Behavioral features: mobile money usage, savings accounts, loans, insurance, etc.

Approach and Methodology

Our approach tests three scenarios:

  1. Models without sample weights - Raw predictions on demographic and behavioral data
  2. Models with sample weights - Population-weighted predictions for demographic representation
  3. Models with SMOTE - Using synthetic minority oversampling to address class imbalance

For each scenario, we compare different classifiers and feature sets to determine the optimal approach for predicting financial exclusion.

LIME Explaining Predictions

The application integrates LIME to explain individual predictions. This allows users to see which features had the most influence on a model's decision, supporting transparency and trust in AI.
